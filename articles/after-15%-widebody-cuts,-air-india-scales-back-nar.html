After 15% widebody cuts, Air India scales back narrowbody flights by 5%, suspends 3 more routes until July 15

Air India, the national carrier of India, has recently announced a significant reduction in its flight operations as part of its ongoing cost-cutting measures. After already cutting 15% of its widebody flights, the airline has now decided to scale back its narrowbody flights by an additional 5% and suspend three more routes until July 15.



This decision comes amidst the challenging times faced by the aviation industry globally due to the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic. With travel restrictions in place and a decrease in passenger demand, airlines are forced to make tough decisions to stay afloat.



Air India's move to reduce its narrowbody flights by 5% is aimed at optimizing its operations and conserving resources. By suspending three more routes until mid-July, the airline is looking to streamline its network and focus on routes that are more profitable and in higher demand.



While these measures may inconvenience some passengers, they are necessary for Air India to navigate through these uncertain times. The airline is not alone in making such cuts, as many other carriers around the world are also adjusting their flight schedules and reducing capacity to adapt to the current situation.



Despite the challenges faced by the aviation industry, Air India remains committed to providing essential air connectivity to passengers. The airline continues to operate on key routes and is taking all necessary precautions to ensure the safety and well-being of its passengers and crew.



As the situation continues to evolve, it is important for travelers to stay informed about any changes to flight schedules and be prepared for potential disruptions. Air India, like many other airlines, is working diligently to adapt to the changing landscape of air travel and is focused on providing a safe and reliable service to its customers.
