Air India'S Hong Kong-Delhi Flight Catches Fire After Landing At Airport - 'Passengers Safe, Aircraft Grounded'

Published on July 22, 2025

On a recent flight from Hong Kong to Delhi, Air India faced a serious incident as their aircraft caught fire after landing at the airport. Despite the frightening ordeal, all passengers and crew members are reported to be safe, and the airline has grounded the aircraft for further investigation.

What Happened?

  • The incident occurred immediately after the Air India flight landed at the airport in Delhi.
  • Witnesses reported seeing flames and smoke coming from the aircraft.
  • Emergency services were quick to respond and evacuate all passengers and crew from the plane.
  • No injuries were reported, and everyone was safely evacuated from the aircraft.

Current Status

Following the incident, Air India has grounded the aircraft for a detailed inspection to determine the cause of the fire. The safety of their passengers and crew members is the airline's top priority, and they are taking all necessary precautions to ensure the well-being of everyone involved.

Investigation Underway

The aviation authorities in India have launched an investigation into the incident to determine the exact cause of the fire. Initial reports suggest that a technical malfunction may have been the reason for the fire, but a thorough examination of the aircraft will provide more clarity on the situation.
