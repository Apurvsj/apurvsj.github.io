More Than Acid Reflux? Doctor Says How To Spot Hiatus Hernia And How To Treat

Published on June 24, 2025

Are you experiencing more than just acid reflux? It might be a hiatus hernia. Dr. Sarah Adams, a leading gastroenterologist, sheds light on how to spot this condition and the best ways to treat it. What is a Hiatus Hernia? A hiatus hernia occurs when part of the stomach pushes up into the chest through the diaphragm. This can lead to symptoms such as heartburn, chest pain, difficulty swallowing, and regurgitation of food. It is often mistaken for acid reflux, but there are key differences between the two conditions. Spotting the Signs of a Hiatus Hernia Dr. Adams highlights some key signs to look out for that may indicate a hiatus hernia: 1. Frequent heartburn that doesn't respond to medication 2. Chest pain that worsens when lying down or bending over 3. Difficulty swallowing or a feeling of food getting stuck in the throat 4. Regurgitation of food or a sour taste in the mouth 5. Shortness of breath or a persistent cough If you are experiencing these symptoms, it is important to consult with your healthcare provider for a proper diagnosis. Diagnosing a Hiatus Hernia Dr. Adams emphasizes the importance of seeking medical advice if you suspect you have a hiatus hernia. Diagnosis often involves a physical examination, imaging tests such as an endoscopy or barium swallow, and possibly a pH test to measure stomach acid levels. Treatment Options for Hiatus Hernia Once diagnosed, there are several treatment options available for hiatus hernia: 1. Lifestyle changes: Dr. Adams recommends avoiding trigger foods that can exacerbate symptoms, such as spicy foods, caffeine, and alcohol. Eating smaller, more frequent meals and maintaining a healthy weight can also help. 2. Medications: Over-the-counter antacids or prescription medications can help manage symptoms of acid reflux associated with a hiatus hernia. 3. Surgery: In severe cases where lifestyle changes and medications are not effective, surgery may be recommended to repair the hernia and prevent further complications. Living with a Hiatus Hernia Dr. Adams stresses the importance of regular follow-up appointments and monitoring of symptoms to manage a hiatus hernia effectively. With the right treatment plan in place, individuals with this condition can lead a healthy and fulfilling life. In conclusion, if you are experiencing persistent symptoms beyond acid reflux, it may be a sign of a hiatus hernia. By recognizing the signs, seeking a proper diagnosis, and following a personalized treatment plan, you can effectively manage this condition and improve your quality of life. Remember to consult with your healthcare provider for individualized advice and support.
