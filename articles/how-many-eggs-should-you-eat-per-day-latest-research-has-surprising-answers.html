How Many Eggs Should You Eat Per Day? Latest Research Has Surprising Answers

Published on July 09, 2025

Are you an egg lover? Do you start your day with a delicious omelette or scramble? If so, you might have wondered at some point: How many eggs is it safe to eat in a day? The latest research on egg consumption might surprise you. Let's dive into the findings and see what experts recommend.

The research findings

  • A recent study published in the Journal of the American Medical Association found that consuming up to one egg per day is not associated with an increased risk of heart disease or stroke.
  • This study contradicts previous beliefs that consuming eggs, due to their cholesterol content, could be harmful to heart health.
  • The research also suggests that eggs are a good source of protein, vitamins, and minerals, making them a nutritious addition to a balanced diet.

Based on these findings, experts now recommend that most people can safely enjoy up to one egg per day as part of a healthy diet.

Factors to consider

While the latest research is encouraging for egg lovers, there are still some factors to consider when it comes to egg consumption:

  • Individual cholesterol levels: If you have high cholesterol or a history of heart disease, it's important to consult with your healthcare provider before increasing your egg intake.
  • Preparation methods: How you cook your eggs can also impact their healthiness. Boiling or poaching eggs is a healthier option compared to frying them in oil.
  • Overall diet: Eating a variety of foods in moderation is key to a balanced diet. Eggs should be enjoyed as part of a diverse meal plan.

FAQ

Q: Can eating eggs every day raise cholesterol levels?

A: The latest research suggests that consuming up to one egg per day is not likely to raise cholesterol levels in most people. However, individuals with pre-existing high cholesterol should consult with a healthcare provider before increasing egg consumption.

Q: Are egg whites healthier than whole eggs?

A: Egg whites are lower in calories and cholesterol than whole eggs, making them a healthier option for individuals watching their cholesterol intake. However, whole eggs contain essential nutrients like vitamin D and choline, so including them in moderation can still be beneficial.
