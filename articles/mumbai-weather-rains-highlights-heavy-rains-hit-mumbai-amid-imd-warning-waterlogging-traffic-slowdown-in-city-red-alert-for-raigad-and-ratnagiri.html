Mumbai Weather & Rains Highlights: Heavy Rains Hit Mumbai Amid Imd Warning; Waterlogging, Traffic Slowdown In City; Red Alert For Raigad And Ratnagiri

Published on July 25, 2025

Heavy rains lashed Mumbai over the past few days, causing waterlogging and traffic slowdown in various parts of the city. The India Meteorological Department (IMD) issued a warning for heavy rainfall in Mumbai and the surrounding areas, including a red alert for Raigad and Ratnagiri districts.

Key Highlights:

  • Mumbai experiences heavy rainfall, leading to waterlogging and traffic disruptions.
  • IMD issues warnings for Mumbai and neighboring districts.
  • Red alert issued for Raigad and Ratnagiri districts.

The heavy downpour in Mumbai has led to significant waterlogging in low-lying areas, causing inconvenience to residents and commuters. Several roads and streets were flooded, leading to traffic jams and delays in public transportation services.

The IMD has predicted that the heavy rainfall is likely to continue over the next few days, urging residents to stay indoors and take necessary precautions. The red alert issued for Raigad and Ratnagiri districts indicates the possibility of extremely heavy rainfall, posing a risk of flooding and landslides in these areas.

Authorities in Mumbai have been working round the clock to pump out water from flooded areas and clear debris from the streets to ensure the safety of the residents. The Brihanmumbai Municipal Corporation (BMC) has set up helplines for citizens to report any emergencies or seek assistance during the heavy rainfall.

FAQs:

A: Residents are advised to stay indoors, avoid unnecessary travel, and keep emergency supplies handy in case of any disruptions in essential services.
