Too 'Woke' For America? Donald Trump Pulls Us Out Of Unesco; Dei Policies, Pro-Palestinian, Pro-China Tilt Prime Reasons

Published on July 22, 2025

Recently, the Trump administration announced its decision to withdraw the United States from the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (Unesco). This move has sparked controversy and raised questions about America's stance on diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) policies as well as its foreign policy priorities.

Reasons for the Withdrawal

  • The Trump administration cited Unesco's alleged anti-Israel bias and its handling of the Palestinian issue as key reasons for the withdrawal.
  • Additionally, the administration criticized Unesco for its perceived pro-China tilt, accusing the organization of being influenced by Beijing's agenda.
  • Furthermore, the administration expressed concerns about Unesco's focus on DEI policies, arguing that they are too politically correct and do not align with American values.

These reasons reflect the Trump administration's broader approach to international relations, which prioritizes America's interests and values over multilateral cooperation.

Implications of the Withdrawal

The withdrawal from Unesco is likely to have far-reaching implications for US foreign policy and global cultural cooperation. It raises questions about the future of international organizations and the role of the US in promoting cultural exchange and education.

Furthermore, the withdrawal may strain the US's relationships with other countries that are members of Unesco, potentially isolating America on the global stage.
