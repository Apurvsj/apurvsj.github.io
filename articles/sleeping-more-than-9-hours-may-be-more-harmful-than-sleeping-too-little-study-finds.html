Sleeping More Than 9 Hours May Be More Harmful Than Sleeping Too Little, Study Finds

Published on July 24, 2025

Getting a good night's sleep is essential for overall health and well-being. However, a recent study has found that sleeping more than 9 hours a night may actually be more harmful than sleeping too little. In this article, we will explore the findings of this study and discuss why excess sleep can have negative consequences on your health.

The Study

A study published in the Journal of the American Heart Association analyzed the sleep patterns of over 10,000 adults over a period of six years. The researchers found that individuals who consistently slept more than 9 hours a night had a significantly higher risk of developing heart disease compared to those who slept 7-8 hours a night. The study also found that excessive sleep was associated with an increased risk of stroke and overall mortality.

Why Excess Sleep Can Be Harmful

  • Disruption of the body's natural circadian rhythm
  • Increased inflammation in the body
  • Higher risk of obesity and metabolic disorders

While it is important to get an adequate amount of sleep each night, oversleeping can disrupt the body's natural circadian rhythm, leading to increased inflammation and a higher risk of obesity and metabolic disorders. Additionally, excessive sleep can be a sign of underlying health issues such as depression, sleep apnea, or thyroid problems.

FAQ

Q: Can oversleeping lead to weight gain?

A: Yes, oversleeping can disrupt your metabolism and lead to weight gain. Excessive sleep can also result in decreased physical activity and poor dietary choices, contributing to obesity.

Q: How much sleep is recommended for adults?

A: The National Sleep Foundation recommends that adults aged 18-64 get 7-9 hours of sleep per night for optimal health and well-being.

Q: What should I do if I consistently oversleep?

A: If you find yourself consistently oversleeping, it is important to speak with a healthcare provider to rule out any underlying health issues. Making lifestyle changes such as establishing a consistent sleep schedule and practicing good sleep hygiene can also help improve your sleep patterns.
