Veda Krishnamurthy Retires From All Forms Of Cricket

Published on July 25, 2025

Indian cricketer Veda Krishnamurthy has announced her retirement from all forms of cricket, bringing an end to a successful career that spanned over a decade. The 28-year-old all-rounder made the announcement on her social media accounts, thanking her fans and teammates for their support throughout her journey.

Highlights of Veda Krishnamurthy's career:

  • Represented India in 48 ODIs, scoring 812 runs at an average of 25.37
  • Played 76 T20Is for India, scoring 875 runs at a strike rate of 100.34
  • Known for her aggressive batting style and athletic fielding

During her career, Veda Krishnamurthy played a crucial role in several memorable victories for the Indian women's cricket team. Her ability to accelerate the scoring rate in the middle overs and provide stability in the lower order made her a valuable asset for the team.

Despite facing personal tragedies with the loss of her mother and sister to COVID-19 in 2020, Veda continued to show resilience and dedication towards the sport she loved. She remained a source of inspiration for many young cricketers, proving that passion and hard work can overcome any adversity.

As Veda Krishnamurthy bids farewell to the cricketing world, she leaves behind a legacy of determination, skill, and sportsmanship that will be remembered by fans and teammates alike.
