Reactor Blast In Telangana: At Least 14 Injured After Fire Breaks Out In Pashamylaram; Rescue Ops Under Way

Published on June 30, 2025

In the early hours of Tuesday, a reactor blast at a chemical factory in Pashamylaram, Telangana, left at least 14 people injured. The blast was followed by a massive fire that quickly engulfed the factory premises, prompting a swift response from emergency services. **Key Facts** - Location: Pashamylaram, Telangana - Incident: Reactor blast followed by fire - Number of injured: At least 14 - Current status: Rescue operations underway - Cause of blast: Under investigation **The Incident** The incident occurred at around 3 a.m. local time when a reactor at the chemical factory exploded, causing a fire to break out. The blast was reportedly heard several kilometers away, and thick plumes of smoke could be seen billowing from the site. **Injuries and Rescue Operations** At least 14 people have been reported injured in the blast, with varying degrees of severity. Emergency services, including fire brigades and ambulances, quickly arrived at the scene to contain the fire and provide medical assistance to the injured. **Current Status** As of now, rescue operations are still underway at the site. Firefighters are working tirelessly to extinguish the flames and prevent any further damage to the surrounding area. The injured have been rushed to nearby hospitals for treatment. **Investigation** The cause of the reactor blast is still under investigation. Authorities are looking into potential safety lapses or negligence that may have led to the incident. The factory has been sealed off for further examination. **Impact on the Community** The blast and subsequent fire have caused panic among the local residents, many of whom have been evacuated from their homes as a precautionary measure. The incident has also raised concerns about the safety standards of industrial units in the area. **Conclusion** The reactor blast in Pashamylaram, Telangana, has once again highlighted the importance of stringent safety measures in industrial settings. As rescue operations continue, our thoughts are with the injured and their families. Authorities must conduct a thorough investigation to ensure that such incidents are prevented in the future.
