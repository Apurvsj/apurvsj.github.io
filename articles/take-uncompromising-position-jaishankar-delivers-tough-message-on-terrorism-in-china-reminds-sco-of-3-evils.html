'Take Uncompromising Position': Jaishankar Delivers Tough Message On Terrorism In China; Reminds Sco Of '3 Evils'

Published on July 15, 2025

Recently, India's External Affairs Minister, S. Jaishankar, delivered a strong message on terrorism in China during the Shanghai Cooperation Organization (SCO) Foreign Ministers' meeting. Jaishankar reminded the SCO member states of the 'three evils' of terrorism, extremism, and separatism, urging them to take an uncompromising position against these threats.

Key Points from Jaishankar's Speech:

  • Emphasized the need for a united front against terrorism in the region
  • Called for a zero-tolerance policy towards states that support and harbor terror groups
  • Highlighted the importance of upholding international conventions and norms in combating terrorism

Jaishankar's tough stance on terrorism in China comes at a time when the issue has become a major concern for countries in the region. With growing tensions and conflicts, it is crucial for nations to stand together in the fight against terrorism.

The External Affairs Minister's message serves as a reminder to the SCO member states to remain vigilant and proactive in addressing the challenges posed by terrorism. By taking an uncompromising position, the countries can work towards ensuring peace and stability in the region.

Implications for Regional Security:

Jaishankar's speech has significant implications for regional security and cooperation. By addressing the issue of terrorism in China head-on, he has set the tone for a more coordinated and effective approach towards tackling this common threat.

It is crucial for the SCO member states to work together in combating terrorism and preventing the spread of extremist ideologies. By taking a firm stand against the 'three evils', the countries can enhance their collective security and promote peace and stability in the region.

Conclusion:

Jaishankar's message on terrorism in China serves as a wake-up call for the SCO member states to prioritize security and cooperation in the fight against terrorism. By reminding them of the 'three evils' and urging them to take an uncompromising position, he has laid the foundation for a more secure and stable region.
